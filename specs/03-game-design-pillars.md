# Game Design Pillars

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Owner: Founder

---

# Purpose

This document defines why RunQuest is fun.

It explains the emotional design behind the gameplay.

Every future mechanic must strengthen one or more pillars defined here.

If a feature weakens these pillars, it should not be implemented.

---

# Core Fantasy

RunQuest allows players to experience the mindset of a runner.

Players are not controlling a character.

Players are making the same strategic decisions that real runners make before and during a race.

The fantasy is:

"What would I do if I were standing on today's starting line?"

---

# Core Emotion

The emotional journey should always follow this pattern.

Curiosity

↓

Preparation

↓

Uncertainty

↓

Tension

↓

Relief

↓

Reflection

↓

Discussion

↓

Anticipation

If a race skips several of these emotions, the experience becomes less memorable.

---

# Pillar 1

## Every Decision Has Consequences

Every decision must influence something.

Examples

Choosing Carbon Shoes

↓

Higher speed

↓

Lower stability

Choosing more water

↓

Better hydration

↓

Heavier weight

Choosing an aggressive strategy

↓

Faster early pace

↓

Higher fatigue later

Players should never feel that a choice was meaningless.

---

# Pillar 2

## Information Is Limited

Players never receive complete information.

The game intentionally hides part of reality.

Examples

Weather forecast

Not exact temperature changes.

Course profile

Not every hill.

Coach advice

Helpful, but not always correct.

The player succeeds by making good decisions despite uncertainty.

---

# Pillar 3

## Failure Must Feel Fair

Failure should never feel random.

After finishing,

players should be able to explain why they failed.

Example

"I skipped hydration."

Good.

"I lost because RNG hated me."

Bad.

---

# Pillar 4

## Preparation Is Gameplay

Preparation is not a menu.

Preparation is the first half of the game.

Choosing equipment,

nutrition,

and pacing strategy

is already playing.

---

# Pillar 5

## Stories Are More Valuable Than Scores

The game should generate stories.

Examples

"I pushed on the hill and completely bonked."

"I ignored the coach and somehow won."

"I carried too much water."

Stories are naturally shared.

Numbers are forgotten.

---

# Pillar 6

## Every Race Is Different

No two Daily Races should feel identical.

Variation comes from

distance,

weather,

terrain,

target,

events,

and hidden conditions.

Not from artificial randomness.

---

# Pillar 7

## One Official Attempt Creates Tension

Players only receive one official attempt.

Because of this,

every decision matters.

Unlimited retries destroy emotional investment.

---

# Pillar 8

## Players Learn Naturally

RunQuest should teach running knowledge without feeling educational.

Players learn through consequences.

Examples

Bonking teaches pacing.

Heat teaches hydration.

Wind teaches drafting.

Trail teaches shoe selection.

No tutorial is more powerful than experience.

---

# Pillar 9

## Discussion Is Gameplay

The race ends.

The discussion begins.

Ideal conversations

"What nutrition did you bring?"

"Did anyone trust today's coach?"

"How did you survive KM 16?"

Discussion extends gameplay beyond the browser.

---

# Pillar 10

## Tomorrow Is The Reward

The reward for finishing today

is seeing tomorrow's challenge.

Players return because they are curious.

Not because they are forced.

---

# What We Will Never Do

The following mechanics weaken the game.

Unlimited retries.

Pay-to-win.

Grinding.

Loot boxes.

Artificial waiting timers.

Energy systems.

Multiple Daily Races.

Random rewards with no meaning.

These mechanics conflict with the identity of RunQuest.

---

# Every New Feature Must Answer

Does it create better decisions?

Does it improve today's story?

Does it increase discussion?

Does it improve tomorrow's curiosity?

If not,

it should not exist.

---

# Final Principle

Players should finish every race thinking

"I know exactly what I should try differently tomorrow."
