# Product Principles

Version: 1.0.0
Status: Draft
Owner: Founder

---

# Purpose

This document defines how product decisions are made.

When multiple solutions exist, these principles should guide the decision.

Features may change.

Technology may change.

These principles should rarely change.

---

# Principle 1

## Daily First

Everything starts with today's Daily Race.

The Daily Race is the product.

Everything else exists to support it.

If a feature distracts from today's race,

it should not be built.

---

# Principle 2

## Story Over Score

Players remember stories.

Players forget numbers.

The game should explain:

Why something happened.

Not only:

What happened.

Example:

Good

"You attacked the hill too early and paid the price."

Bad

"Energy -14"

---

# Principle 3

## Decisions Over Reflexes

Winning should come from thinking.

Not tapping faster.

Not clicking more.

Not grinding longer.

Every meaningful outcome should be the result of one or more player decisions.

---

# Principle 4

## Simplicity Over Features

The simplest solution that creates the desired emotion should always be chosen.

Do not build systems simply because other games have them.

Every additional feature increases cognitive load.

---

# Principle 5

## Discussion Is Success

The ideal player conversation is:

"What strategy did you choose?"

Not:

"What level are you?"

Good gameplay naturally creates discussion.

---

# Principle 6

## Learn Without Teaching

RunQuest should never feel like a textbook.

Players should learn naturally through consequences.

Instead of explaining hydration,

let dehydration create a memorable story.

---

# Principle 7

## One Meaningful Attempt

Players should care about today's race because they only have one official attempt.

Scarcity creates emotion.

Unlimited retries remove tension.

---

# Principle 8

## Curiosity Beats Rewards

Players should return because they are curious.

Not because they are forced.

Examples of healthy motivation:

"I wonder what tomorrow's race is."

Examples of unhealthy motivation:

"I need to collect another chest."

---

# Principle 9

## Content Over Systems

Adding 100 interesting Daily Races is more valuable than adding one complicated progression system.

The product grows primarily through content.

Not mechanics.

---

# Principle 10

## Build for Retention, Not Session Length

A player who returns tomorrow is more valuable than a player who spends one hour today.

Never optimize for endless sessions.

Optimize for daily habits.

---

# Principle 11

## Honest Difficulty

The game should be difficult because decisions are difficult.

Not because information is unfair.

Players may not know everything,

but every outcome must feel explainable afterwards.

---

# Principle 12

## Every Screen Has One Job

Each screen should have a single purpose.

Home

→ Create curiosity.

Briefing

→ Prepare the player.

Preparation

→ Make decisions.

Race

→ Experience consequences.

Result

→ Reflect.

Share

→ Tell the story.

Never mix responsibilities.

---

# Principle 13

## Remove Before Adding

Whenever a new feature is proposed,

first ask:

"What existing complexity can we remove?"

The product should become simpler over time,

not heavier.

---

# Principle 14

## AI Is Part of the Team

AI is not only a code generator.

AI participates in:

- product decisions
- architecture
- implementation
- testing
- documentation
- review

All AI-generated work must follow the Constitution.

---

# Principle 15

## Build Small, Learn Fast

Release the smallest possible version.

Observe.

Learn.

Improve.

Avoid building features based only on assumptions.

---

# Product Decision Checklist

Before implementing any feature,

ask:

Does this improve today's Daily Race?

Does this create better decisions?

Does this increase curiosity?

Does this create discussion?

Does this improve tomorrow's retention?

If the answer is mostly "No",

do not build it.

---

# Final Principle

RunQuest is not trying to become the biggest running game.

RunQuest is trying to become the first website runners open every morning.
