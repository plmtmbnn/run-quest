# RunQuest Vision

Version: 1.0.0
Status: Draft
Owner: Founder

---

# Purpose

This document defines why RunQuest exists.

It explains the long-term vision of the product and serves as the foundation for every future product decision.

This document is intentionally technology-independent.

---

# Vision

To become the daily ritual for runners around the world by transforming running knowledge into short, meaningful decision-based experiences.

RunQuest does not aim to replace running.

RunQuest exists to improve how people think about running.

---

# Mission

Help runners make smarter decisions before they make faster runners.

Every Daily Race should teach something.

Even failure should become a learning experience.

---

# Product Statement

RunQuest is a web-based daily decision game inspired by the real-world thinking process of runners.

Players are presented with one official Daily Race every day.

They prepare.

They make decisions.

They experience the consequences.

They learn.

They share.

They return tomorrow.

---

# Product Identity

RunQuest is NOT

- A running simulator
- A fitness tracker
- A GPS running application
- An endless mobile game
- A clicker game
- A racing game

RunQuest IS

- A daily ritual
- A strategic decision game
- A storytelling experience
- A learning platform disguised as a game
- A social conversation starter

---

# Product Pillars

Every feature should strengthen one or more of these pillars.

## Pillar 1 — Daily Ritual

The player should naturally want to open RunQuest every day.

The Daily Race is the heartbeat of the product.

---

## Pillar 2 — Meaningful Decisions

Winning should come from making good decisions.

Not from reaction speed.

Not from grinding.

---

## Pillar 3 — Learn Through Play

Players should gradually understand concepts such as:

- pacing
- hydration
- nutrition
- race strategy
- weather adaptation
- energy management

without reading tutorials.

---

## Pillar 4 — Storytelling

Every race should generate a memorable story.

The story matters more than the score.

Players should remember:

"I started too aggressively."

instead of

"I finished in 51 minutes."

---

## Pillar 5 — Social Conversation

Sharing results should naturally create discussion.

Examples:

"What shoes did you choose?"

"Did anyone bring enough water?"

"Coach's advice fooled me today."

---

# Product Philosophy

The product should feel calm.

Not noisy.

No flashing effects.

No unnecessary animations.

No reward explosions.

No addictive tricks.

Instead,

players return because they are curious about tomorrow.

---

# Emotional Goals

RunQuest should create:

Curiosity before the race.

Tension during the race.

Reflection after the race.

Discussion after sharing.

Anticipation for tomorrow.

---

# What Success Looks Like

A successful player says:

"I can't wait to see tomorrow's race."

not

"I need to grind more."

---

# Long-Term Vision

Future versions may include:

- seasonal events
- race archives
- coaching insights
- friends
- clubs
- global leaderboards
- race builder
- AI-generated races

However,

the Daily Race must always remain the center of the experience.

---

# Things We Will Protect

No pay-to-win.

No energy system.

No loot boxes.

No mandatory advertisements.

No artificial waiting timers.

No gameplay that rewards endless grinding.

---

# Decision Filter

Every future feature must answer:

Does it strengthen the Daily Ritual?

Does it create meaningful decisions?

Does it generate memorable stories?

Does it encourage conversation?

If not,

it should not be built.

---

# One Sentence Vision

RunQuest is where every runner begins today's race before tomorrow's run.
