# RunQuest Glossary

Version: 1.0.0
Status: Draft

---

## Purpose

This glossary defines the official vocabulary of RunQuest.

Every documentation, prompt, source code, UI copy, and future AI interaction must use these terms consistently.

If multiple words could describe the same concept, only the official term defined here should be used.

---

# Core Concepts

## Daily Race

The single official race available to every player each day.

Every player receives the exact same Daily Race.

There is only one Daily Race per calendar day.

---

## Official Attempt

A player's one and only valid attempt for a Daily Race.

An Official Attempt produces the player's permanent result for that day.

Phase 0 allows only one Official Attempt.

---

## Briefing

The pre-race information shown before the player starts.

The Briefing intentionally provides incomplete information.

It prepares the player for decision making.

---

## Preparation

The phase where players make strategic decisions before the race begins.

Preparation may include:

- Shoes
- Nutrition
- Race Strategy
- Mental Focus

Preparation ends when the race starts.

---

## Simulation

The deterministic engine that calculates the race outcome.

Simulation is invisible to players.

Players only experience its results.

Simulation must never contain UI logic.

---

## Checkpoint

A decision point during the race.

Players are presented with one or more choices.

Every checkpoint changes the simulation state.

---

## Decision

A choice made by the player.

Every Decision must have meaningful consequences.

No Decision should exist purely for decoration.

---

## Event

A situation that occurs during the race.

Events may be triggered by:

- Race configuration
- Weather
- Hidden stats
- Previous decisions

Events are not always random.

---

## Hidden Stats

Internal variables that influence the simulation.

Examples:

- Energy
- Hydration
- Glycogen
- Fatigue
- Confidence
- Muscle Condition
- Mental Focus

Players should never see exact values.

---

## Story

The narrative generated from the player's decisions.

The Story is the emotional outcome of a race.

Players should remember the Story more than the numbers.

---

## Result

The final outcome of the Daily Race.

A Result includes:

- Finish Time
- Rating
- Story Summary
- Performance Analysis

---

## Share Card

A visual summary generated after completing a Daily Race.

Its purpose is social sharing.

It should encourage curiosity from non-players.

---

# Race Elements

## Weather

Environmental conditions affecting the race.

Examples:

- Hot
- Cold
- Windy
- Rainy
- Humid

Weather influences strategy.

---

## Course

The route profile of the race.

Examples:

- Flat
- Rolling
- Hilly
- Trail
- Urban

---

## Aid Station

A location where players may choose to hydrate or refuel.

Aid Stations create strategic opportunities.

---

## Nutrition

Resources consumed during a race.

Examples:

- Gel
- Sports Drink
- Water
- Salt Tablet

Nutrition influences Hidden Stats.

---

## Strategy

The pacing approach selected before the race.

Examples:

- Conservative
- Even Pace
- Negative Split
- Aggressive

---

## Mental Focus

The player's chosen mindset.

Mental Focus affects how the simulation reacts to difficult situations.

---

# Product Concepts

## Daily Ritual

The primary product philosophy.

RunQuest is designed to become part of a player's daily routine.

---

## Retention

A player's willingness to return tomorrow.

Retention is the primary success metric.

---

## Shareability

The likelihood that a player voluntarily shares their result.

Shareability is a core product goal.

---

## Discussion

Conversations generated by different player decisions.

Discussion is considered a successful gameplay outcome.

---

# Technical Concepts

## Simulation Engine

The core deterministic engine.

It accepts:

- Race Configuration
- Player Decisions

It returns:

- Race Timeline
- Result

The Simulation Engine must remain independent from UI.

---

## Race Configuration

A JSON document describing today's race.

The Simulation Engine reads Race Configuration.

It never modifies it.

---

## Player Profile

Locally stored player information.

Phase 0 includes:

- Player ID
- Language
- Settings
- Progress
- History

---

## Local Player ID

A UUID generated on first launch.

This identifier represents an anonymous player.

It must never change unless LocalStorage is cleared.

---

## Storage Version

The version number of LocalStorage schema.

Used for future migrations.

---

# Forbidden Terms

To keep terminology consistent, these words should not be used.

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|--------|-------------|
| Level | Daily Race |
| Mission | Daily Race |
| Stage | Daily Race |
| Avatar | Player |
| HP | Energy |
| XP | Progress (future) |
| Inventory | Equipment (future) |
| Skin | Theme (future) |

---

# Naming Convention

Use nouns consistently.

Good:

Daily Race

Preparation

Checkpoint

Decision

Simulation

Result

Story

Avoid synonyms unless officially introduced.

---

# Final Rule

If a new gameplay concept is introduced,

it must first be added to this glossary

before implementation begins.
